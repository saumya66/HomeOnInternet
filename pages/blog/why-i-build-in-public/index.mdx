There is a version of me that would wait until everything was perfect before showing it to anyone. The code had to be clean, the idea had to be validated, the product had to be polished. That version of me shipped almost nothing.

Building in public fixed that.

## What "building in public" actually means

It does not mean tweeting every commit or turning your work into a performance. It means making your process visible — the decisions, the dead ends, the things you are figuring out as you go.

For me it looks like:
- Writing a post about something I just learned, before I feel like an expert on it
- Sharing a project while it is still rough
- Talking about what is not working, not just what is

The bar is low on purpose. If I wait until I am confident, I wait forever.

## The real reason most people don't do it

It is not laziness. It is the fear of being wrong in public.

When you share something unfinished, you are exposed. Someone might point out a mistake. Someone might have already built it better. Someone might not care at all — which, honestly, feels worse.

But here is what I have found: the people who matter do not judge you for being early. They respect the fact that you are moving.

## What I have gotten from it

The feedback loop is faster. When you share what you are building, people tell you things you would never figure out alone. Not always useful things — but often exactly what you needed to hear.

I have also found that writing about something forces me to actually understand it. You cannot bluff your way through a blog post the way you can in your own head.

And occasionally, someone reads something and says *"I was thinking about the same thing"* — and that is worth every awkward post.

## How to start

Pick one thing you are working on right now. Write one paragraph about it — what it is, why you are building it, what is hard about it. Post it somewhere. That's it.

You do not need a newsletter or a following or a polished website. You just need to start the habit of making your work visible.

The rest follows.
